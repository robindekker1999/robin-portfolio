Responsive multi device design: Martin Eats

Creating a multi device experience to help users with the everyday task of feeding your cat, even when you're not home.

  • Responsive multi device design
  • App design
  • UI/UX design
  • Creative problem solving

Designing a multi device app to ease everyday struggles

Everyone loves their pets, but feeding them on time every day can be a challenge. We have busy lives outside of our pets, often being away from home when we should be feeding our cats. To make life a bit easier, the automatic cat feeder was created! But to truly utilise the cat feeder to its full potential, you need an app to accomodate that.

The MartinEats app helps the user keep an eye on their pet while they are away from home. With the app you can either set an automatic feeding schedule tailored to your pet or manually feed them remotely with just a tap on the screen.

In addition to your phone, this app is also accesible from your watch. With just a quick notification, you will be reminded to feed the cat at any given time.

Designed to keep track of your cat's food and health

The main feature of this app is being able to feed your cat from a distance. However, there's more to it. Users can create a profile for their cat, including a personalised feeding schedule. Additionally, every time the cat approaches the feeder, it tracks their body weight and the amount of food consumed. If the cat requires special medication, it can be included in the food. In this way, the app also monitors the cat's health.

The only task left for the user is to ensure the feeder contains enough food. Even this issue can be easily managed, as the app regularly updates the user about the feeder's status. This ensures both cat and owner are content and your little friend is never left hungry again.
